On the Future of Programming

One area that has constantly interested me in CS is how we express our ideas using technology. Although, a majority of society I stuck with the notion of programming—from what has been brough up from the "dark ages" of the 1960s—that being typing in some sort of text editor or terminal, compiling code, juggling everything in your head.

The idea is that instead of treating code like this arcane magic, we can instead move towards "humane" mediums that require less tedium and allow for greater explination and exploration.
